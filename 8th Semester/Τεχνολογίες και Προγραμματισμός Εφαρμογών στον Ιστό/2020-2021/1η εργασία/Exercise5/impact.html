On SolarWinds

The attack had a big and long lasting financial impact on the company. It's easy to see how the outbreak of the news about the attack affected the price of SolarWind's stock (SWI). According to stock market watch websites such as www.marketwatch.com on December 13 2020, the exact date when Reuters disclosed the nature of the attack, the SWI stock began to plummet as it dropped nearly 23% in one week. Since then the stock has slowly risen in price, although the effects of this disclosure can still be seen today as it has lost a lot of its former value. One of the many reasons why this is could be because the attack shook the shareholders' and the clients' faith in the company which made many of them withdraw their funds and uninstall their software respectably.

On the U.S. Government & Infastructure

It's not a surprise that this particular cyberattack has shaken the US to its core. A global super-power such as the USA has been enduring such attacks for years be it from the inside, or from its competitors, particularly China and Russia. But why is this attack any different?
First of all, Orion and similar affected products were installed in many U.S. agencies including the U.S. military, the State Department, the National Security Agency, and the Office of President of the United States. We can therefore estimate what kind of information could have been leaked and how it was going to be used in the worst case senario. The fact that the attack was discovered right before the elections for the US presidency also raised major concern over whether private data about the candidates was stolen and how this could affect the outcome.
